Fedor Kurekhin, born in St. Petersburg, is a director and writer based in Berlin. As a director, he specializes in the fusion of genres, exploring post-memory, and incorporating elements of his own biography. In his performances, he delves into local contexts and human relationships in the places where he works, constructing mythologies within them. Following this, he creates cross-genre narrative stories, immersing them in his biography through a total LO-FI theater approach.

· As a film director he works with archives, DIY films, AI, and immigration context.

· As a theater director, he often works in non-theatrical spaces — the forest, a winter beach, or a rooftop.

· As a writer, he engages with Russian toxic masculinity in "Disappearance" and "Men's Tears", and explores war and immigration in "Landscape of Lost Time" (Freedom Letters, 2025).

· Working as a Director at Sergey Kuryokhin Contemporary Art Center, Saint-Petersburg.

· 2025 — Founded independent DIY film company Wrong Movement.

· 2017/2018 — Director of the Sergey Kuryokhin Contemporary Art Award ceremony at the Big Drama Theatre (BDT), and other venues including Finnish Bay (2022).

· 2021/2022 — Blackbox Residence at The Meyerhold Center, Moscow.

· 2014 — Published novel Disappearance (Amphora); republished 2023.

· Participated in the playwright festival Lyubimovka: Playwriting Against War with the play Free Paths.

· Published Mixed Feeling — theoretic-fiction about the experience in Russian theater.

· Film 26 shown at VideoForma 7. W or Vanishing shown at Durchgang/KücheFilms.
